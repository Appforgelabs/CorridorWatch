📖 How the Corridor Method Works

The Corridor Method — developed by AJ Investment Research (@alojoh) — answers: is this stock cheap or expensive relative to how the market has historically priced it?

  • Observed forward P/E: At each point in time, the implied forward P/E = stock price ÷ NTM EPS estimate.
  • Rolling statistics: Over a lookback window (90-day or 1-year), compute the median and standard deviation of those daily observed multiples.
  • 5-band corridor: Five lines — ±1.5σ outer (red), ±1.0σ inner (green), median (gray dashed) — bound four fill zones. Outer zones (±1.0σ to ±1.5σ) are shaded red; inner zones (median to ±1.0σ) are shaded green.
  • Signal reading: Price above upper red line = historically expensive, least attractive entry. Price in green zone = fair value. Price below lower red line = historically cheap, most attractive entry.
  • Price targets: 12M and 24M targets = median multiple × forward EPS at those future dates (based on projected earnings growth).
  • AJ's edge: Proprietary EPS estimates more accurate than Wall Street consensus (3% vs 7% error on AAPL; near-perfect on ORCL). More accurate EPS → more accurate corridor → better entry signals.

Formula: EPS (NTM) × P/E (NTM) = Share Price (implied)
