📖 How the Corridor Method Works

The Corridor Method is AJ's proprietary valuation framework. It answers: is this stock cheap or expensive relative to how the market has historically priced it?

  • Forward P/E observed: At each point in time, implied P/E = stock price ÷ NTM EPS estimate.
  • Rolling median + σ: Over the past 1 year (252 days), compute the median and standard deviation of those daily observed multiples.
  • Corridor bands: Upper = (median + 1.5σ) × EPS. Lower = (median − 1.5σ) × EPS. These are the "normal" price range.
  • 90-Day corridor: Same method using only the last 90 days — more sensitive to recent sentiment shifts.
  • Signal: Trading above upper band = historically expensive = least attractive entry. Below lower band = historically cheap = most attractive entry.
  • Long-term upside is separate — combines expected earnings growth × future multiple. A stock can be poor near-term (above corridor) but still have huge long-term upside (e.g. PLTR).
  • AJ's edge: More accurate EPS estimates than Wall Street consensus (3% error vs 7% on AAPL; near-perfect on ORCL) → more accurate corridor bounds → better signals.
